
\section{Mathematical Notation}

% Simple equation
You can write simple equations:
\begin{equation}
y_i = \beta_0 + \beta_1 x_i + \varepsilon_i
\end{equation}

% Algebra expressions
Algebraic expressions can be written inline or displayed:
\[
(a + b)^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2
\]

% Summation notation
Summation is easy with \texttt{\textbackslash sum}:
\[
\bar{x} = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i
\]

% Matrices
Matrices are displayed with the \texttt{bmatrix} environment:
\[
X = 
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & x_{11} & x_{12} \\
1 & x_{21} & x_{22} \\
\vdots & \vdots & \vdots \\
1 & x_{n1} & x_{n2}
\end{bmatrix}, \quad
\beta =
\begin{bmatrix}
\beta_0 \\ \beta_1 \\ \beta_2
\end{bmatrix}
\]

% Matrix multiplication example
Then the linear model in matrix form:
\[
Y = X \beta + \varepsilon
\]

% Vectors
Vectors can be expressed in bold or with arrow notation:
\[
\mathbf{y} =
\begin{bmatrix}
y_1 \\ y_2 \\ \vdots \\ y_n
\end{bmatrix}, \quad
\vec{x}_i =
\begin{bmatrix}
x_{i1} \\ x_{i2}
\end{bmatrix}
\]

% Algebraic manipulation example
Solving for OLS estimator:
\[
\hat{\beta} = (X'X)^{-1} X'Y
\]
